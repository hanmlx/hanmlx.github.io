Study on a Cancer Treatment Technique

Team: Matteo, Mathias, Macram, William

Date: March - June 2024

This project involved a study on the effectiveness of a specific treatment technique for cancer, using data provided by an American hospital. We learned to organize our work using a schedule, which was essential to meet deadlines.

From March to June, my team and I conducted an exciting project aimed at evaluating the effectiveness of a specific treatment technique for cancer.

Main Objective

Our goal was to determine whether a given treatment technique was effective for cancer patients.

Methodology and Tools

To carry out this project, we used the R programming language for data cleaning and analysis.

Key Steps

  1. Organization and Planning: We set up a detailed schedule to manage our tasks and meet deadlines.
  2. Data Cleaning: We identified and corrected errors and anomalies to ensure reliable data.
  3. Statistical Analysis: We applied statistical methods to evaluate correlations between the treatment technique and patient outcomes.

Challenges and Solutions

Remote Work Synchronizing files caused problems, making collaboration more complex.

Statistical Analysis: Graphical analysis, especially heatmaps, initially presented challenges. With the help of our professor, we overcame these obstacles and deepened our understanding.

Skills Developed

  • R Programming
  • Statistics
  • Collaboration and Organization

Results and Conclusions

The results showed that the technique reduced mortality by 14% in patients who had suffered a heart attack, indicating its effectiveness for this group. However, for patients with vascular problems or who had had a heart attack, the treatment seemed to increase mortality. Overall, although patients treated with this technique had less favorable initial prognoses, the treatment showed no significant differences in variables like blood pressure and heart rate. These results highlight the importance of carefully selecting patients for this procedure.

Design and Implementation of a Database for the LB AXA BOS Banking Group

Date: April - June 2024

This project involved the design and implementation of a database for the LB AXA BOS banking group, following their merger. We learned to organize our work using a schedule, which was essential to meet deadlines.

From April to June, our team carried out an exciting project aimed at structuring the data, importing it, validating it, and addressing the issue of forecasting staffing needs.

Main Objective

Our goal was to structure the data, import it, validate it, and address the issue of forecasting staffing needs. The project aimed to increase productivity by 3% in the workforce.

Methodology and Tools

For this project, we used the following tools:

  • Microsoft Access for designing and managing the database.
  • Excel for preparing and transforming data.
  • SQL for queries and data analysis.

Key Steps

  1. Organization and Planning: We set up a detailed schedule to manage our tasks and meet deadlines.
  2. Analysis of Raw Files: We analyzed raw files to identify unnecessary columns and data to transform.
  3. Data Transformation: We transformed data formats for easier import (e.g., converting text formats to numbers).
  4. Data Loading: We imported the data into Access and checked for potential errors.
  5. Identification and Correction of Anomalies: We identified and corrected several anomalies, such as closed establishments with ongoing operations.
  6. Results Analysis: We calculated productivity rates and forecasts for staffing to achieve a 3% increase.

Challenges and Solutions

Remote Work: Synchronizing files caused problems, making collaboration more complex. We used online collaboration tools to share files and updates in real time.

Statistical Analysis: Analyzing the data, especially complex queries, initially posed challenges. Thanks to our professor's guidance and documentation, we overcame these obstacles and deepened our understanding.

Skills Developed

  • SQL
  • Microsoft Access
  • Excel
  • Collaboration and Organization

Results and Conclusions

The results show that the goal of a 3% increase in productivity was not achieved in nearly all cases. We proposed specific adjustments for each product category and commercial division. Our recommendations include:

  • Reducing staffing in categories with low productivity growth.
  • Transferring staff between categories to optimize resources.
  • Optimizing processes in categories showing negative productivity growth rates.

Collect Auto

Team: Matteo CAI, Diego CASAS, Leo UNITE

Date: December 2024 - January 2025

This project involved an in-depth analysis of online purchasing behavior, using data from various sources to identify the largest consumers of online products.

Throughout this project, we used various data sources to get a comprehensive view of consumer profiles and the most popular products.

Main Objective

Our goal was to answer the question: "Who are the largest consumers of online products?" We analyzed the data to identify consumption trends and the most popular products.

Methodology and Tools

For this project, we used the following tools:

  • Dataset Search to find relevant external datasets.
  • Kaggle to obtain datasets such as the Perfume E-Commerce Dataset 2024 and the E-commerce Customer Behavior Dataset.
  • Power BI for data visualization and analysis.

Key Steps

  1. Organization and Planning: We set up a detailed schedule to manage our tasks and meet deadlines.
  2. Data Extraction: We extracted data from various sources, including Kaggle and Dataset Search.
  3. Data Transformation: We cleaned and transformed the data to make it usable.
  4. Data Loading: We loaded the data into a data warehouse for structured analysis.
  5. Analysis and Visualization: We used Power BI to create visualizations and dashboards.

Challenges and Solutions

Data Integration: Merging data from different sources posed challenges regarding format and granularity. We used common keys to link the tables and ensure consistent analysis.

Statistical Analysis: Data analysis, especially creating complex visualizations, initially posed challenges. Using Power BI and documentation, we overcame these obstacles and deepened our understanding.

Skills Developed

  • Using Kaggle and Dataset Search
  • Data Cleaning and Transformation
  • Data Visualization with Power BI
  • Collaboration and Organization

Results and Conclusions

The results show that the most popular online products are perfumes, video games, and electronics. The analyzed data also revealed distinct consumption trends based on geographic regions and demographic groups.

The visualizations created helped identify the most active consumer segments and the best-selling products. These analyses are essential for optimizing sales strategies and improving the customer experience.
